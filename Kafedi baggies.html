Caramel Flavor: Instead of artificial flavors, consider natural alternatives. Finely grinding dehydrated dates, mesquite powder, or lucuma powder can create rich, caramel-like notes that complement coffee.

Vanilla Flavor: For a pure vanilla taste, source high-quality dehydrated vanilla beans. Be aware this can be a premium ingredient, so budget accordingly. Grind them to a fine powder for the best distribution.

Sensory Analysis: Use a blind tasting method (participants don't know which sample is which) to avoid bias. Create a simple scoring sheet to rate aroma, flavor, and body on a scale of 1-5 for quantifiable feedback.

Perforation Tests: The goal is to balance extraction (more holes) with integrity (fewer holes). Brew over a white cup to easily spot any sediment leakage. Test a grid pattern vs. a random distribution of holes.

Weight Lock-in: Finalizing the gram weight is a critical milestone. This exact weight will inform the dimensions of the final bag, the design of your packaging, and the calibration of any future automated filling equipment. It ensures every customer gets a consistent product.

Bag Shape: The pyramid (sachet) shape is popular because it allows the coffee grounds to tumble and expand, promoting better water circulation and a more even extraction. Square bags are more space-efficient for packing but may cause grounds to clump in corners.
